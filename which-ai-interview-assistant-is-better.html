Which AI Interview Assistant is Better? Final Round AI vs CloakAI vs Yoodli

✍ CloakAI Team 📅 2026-06-15 ⏱ 7 min read

If you are researching which AI interview assistant is better, you have probably already seen the big names — Final Round AI, Yoodli, Interview Warmup, and others. This article cuts through the marketing claims and gives you an honest comparison based on what actually matters: does it work during a real interview, and will your interviewer know you are using it?

⚡ Bottom Line Up Front

Among dedicated AI interview assistants, CloakAI is better for live interviews — it is the only tool that is genuinely invisible to screenshare while providing real-time AI answers. For pure practice and coaching, Yoodli and Google Interview Warmup have their place. But if you want help during the actual interview, the comparison is not close.

What Makes an AI Interview Assistant "Better"?

The word "better" means different things depending on your goal. Here is the framework we use to evaluate every tool in this comparison:

  • Live interview capability: Does it work during the actual interview, or only in practice?
  • Screenshare invisibility: Can the interviewer see it if you share your screen?
  • Answer quality: Are the answers personalised, accurate, and interview-appropriate?
  • Setup friction: How much effort is required to use it effectively?
  • Cost: Is the pricing sustainable for a job seeker?
  • Platform compatibility: Does it work on Zoom, Teams, Google Meet?

Detailed Tool Comparison

Final Round AI

Final Round AI is one of the most well-known interview AI tools. It offers real-time suggestions and claims to help during live interviews. However, several concerns make it difficult to recommend without reservation:

  • The interface can be visible on screenshare depending on your setup, which has been flagged by users on Reddit and review platforms
  • Pricing starts at $96/month for the features that matter — extremely expensive for a job seeker
  • The AI responses are sometimes generic and not personalised to the candidate's specific background
  • Setup requires significant configuration before it works reliably

Better than CloakAI? No — higher price, higher visibility risk, less personalisation.

Yoodli

Yoodli is a speech coaching tool, not an answer generator. It analyses how you speak — your pacing, filler words, eye contact, and overall communication style. This is genuinely valuable for candidates who need to improve their delivery, not their content. However, Yoodli does not answer interview questions. It tells you that you said "um" 14 times and spoke too fast, but it does not help you explain why you want to leave your current job or how you handled a difficult project. It is a communication coach, not an interview assistant.

Better than CloakAI? Different category. Use Yoodli for communication training, CloakAI for content and real-time answers.

Google Interview Warmup

Completely free, completely honest about what it is: a practice tool. You answer questions out loud, it transcribes your answer, and gives you feedback on the words you used. It does not give you the answer — it evaluates the answer you give. Useful for building comfort with speaking about your experience, but limited in depth and not useful during real interviews.

Better than CloakAI? No — it is a practice tool with no live interview functionality.

Metaview

Metaview is primarily aimed at interviewers, not candidates. It records and summarises interviews from the hiring team's perspective. Some candidates use it to record and review their own interviews after the fact, but it has no live assistance capability and is not designed for job seekers in the first place.

Better than CloakAI? Completely different tool — not a fair comparison.

CloakAI

CloakAI was built from the ground up to answer the question: "How can a candidate get AI support during a live interview without the interviewer knowing?" Every feature decision flows from that single goal.

The architecture is fundamentally different from every other tool:

  • It runs as a system overlay using Windows content protection — the same technology used for DRM-protected content. No screen recording software at any level can capture it.
  • It uses microphone and system audio capture to hear both you and the interviewer, so it understands the full context of the conversation.
  • You paste your resume and target role once. Every answer CloakAI generates is grounded in your actual experience — the system does not just give generic templates.
  • The Screen Scan feature lets you point at any coding problem on your screen and get a solution, without typing anything.
  • The entire interface is controlled by keyboard shortcuts, so your mouse and hands remain in normal interview position throughout.

CloakAI vs the Current Market Leaders: Parakeet AI, Chiku AI & LockedIn AI

The AI interview tool market has grown significantly in the past year. Three names have emerged as the most discussed alternatives in 2026 — Parakeet AI, Chiku AI, and LockedIn AI. Here is an honest breakdown of each, and how CloakAI compares on the things that actually matter.

Parakeet AI

Parakeet AI is one of the most widely marketed AI interview tools globally, claiming 1.5 million users (self-reported). It works in real time on Zoom, Teams, and Meet with support for 52+ languages. The product is credit-based — 0.5 credits per 30-minute session — with no monthly subscription. Credits never expire.

Where it falls short for Indian candidates: The entry price is $29.50 (approximately ₹2,650) for 3 interview credits. That is over 5× the cost of CloakAI's entry plan for a similar amount of usage. Parakeet also has no free plan — you cannot test it before paying. Its privacy claims ("undetectable") are prominent in marketing but rely on the same mechanisms most AI tools claim, without the OS-level content protection CloakAI uses on Windows. Answers are not as deeply personalised to your specific resume as CloakAI's.

Parakeet AI vs CloakAI verdict: Parakeet is a credible tool with strong multilingual support, but for Indian job seekers it is significantly overpriced. CloakAI offers the same core real-time functionality at a fraction of the cost, with a genuinely free 20-minute trial and deeper resume personalisation.

Chiku AI

Chiku AI is the most direct Indian market competitor to CloakAI. It positions itself as "69% cheaper than Parakeet AI" and targets Indian candidates with a "Desi Mode" — answers tuned to how Indian engineers actually speak in interviews. It supports 52+ languages, handles coding rounds, and uses a credit-based model starting at ₹1,199 (+GST).

Where it falls short: The entry price of ₹1,199 + GST (approximately ₹1,415 all-in) still works out far costlier per hour than CloakAI's ₹999 for 3 hours (₹333 per AI hour). More critically, Chiku AI is a browser-based tool — it runs in Chrome rather than as a native desktop application. Browser-based tools are significantly more detectable during screen sharing because browser windows appear in screen capture by default and tab-switching is visible. CloakAI uses native Windows content protection at the OS level, which browser-based tools fundamentally cannot replicate. Chiku also has a smaller user base (~10K candidates) and limited third-party review presence.

Chiku AI vs CloakAI verdict: Chiku AI gets the India-first positioning right, but the browser-based architecture is a real limitation on screenshare protection. CloakAI is cheaper, genuinely invisible at the OS level, and has a proper 20-minute free trial. For Indian candidates, CloakAI is the better value by both price and reliability.

LockedIn AI

LockedIn AI is a broad career platform with 14+ tools — resume builder, job auto-apply, headshot generator, and an interview copilot. It has a free tier and premium plans starting at $49.99/month (approximately ₹4,200/month). The platform claims an ultra-fast 116ms response time and offers a unique "LockedIn Duo" feature that lets a trusted contact assist you during your interview in real time.

Where it falls short: At $49.99/month, it is one of the most expensive options on the market for regular use. More importantly, user reviews note that LockedIn AI is browser-based and has reported visibility issues — multiple independent reviewers have flagged that interviewers can see the interface when tabs are switched or the screen is shared. The breadth of features (14+ tools) is impressive on paper but means the live interview copilot itself is not the primary focus. CloakAI does one thing and does it properly: invisible real-time AI during live interviews.

LockedIn AI vs CloakAI verdict: If you want a broad career platform with many tools, LockedIn AI covers a lot of ground. If you want the most reliable invisible live interview AI at the lowest cost, CloakAI wins clearly — no subscription, no visibility risk, no monthly commitment, and a free trial.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI interview assistant is better — CloakAI or Final Round AI?+
CloakAI is better for live interviews for three key reasons: it is invisible to screenshare (Final Round AI has reported visibility issues), it is significantly cheaper (₹999 for 3 hours vs $96/month), and its answers are personalised based on your specific resume rather than generic templates.

So when asked 'tell me about a time you handled conflict', the answer will reference your actual background.
Is there a free AI interview assistant?+
Yes. CloakAI offers a 20-minute free trial with full access to all features. Google Interview Warmup is completely free for practice sessions. Yoodli has a free tier for communication coaching. For live interviews with screenshare protection, only CloakAI provides this capability.
How does CloakAI stay hidden from the interviewer?+
CloakAI uses Windows content protection technology — the same system used to protect DRM content like Netflix. This works on Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, OBS, and any other capture software.
