🧠 Why CloakAI Exists

Modern job interviews are brutal. Companies use HackerRank, LeetCode, system design whiteboards, and behavioural frameworks all compressed into 45-minute windows that rarely reflect the day-to-day reality of the role.

Most candidates spend weeks preparing, reading documentation, and practising problems they may never encounter. A single bad day anxiety, a hard question, a topic gap can derail months of preparation and a legitimate career opportunity.

We believe preparation tools shouldn't end when the interview starts. CloakAI is the reference layer that stays with you throughout the process helping you articulate what you know, recover when you're stuck, and present your best self under pressure.

It's not about faking expertise. It's about making sure your knowledge and preparation actually show up when it matters most.

🛠 What Makes CloakAI Different

CloakAI was built from scratch with one obsessive focus: make the experience completely invisible and completely instant. From the first day we started building, we refused to use shortcuts. Every component was designed to fail gracefully, work reliably, and never draw attention to itself during the most important moments of your career.

The screenshare invisibility is real — not a visual trick, not a black window overlay, not something that breaks when you share your whole screen instead of a window. It works the same way across every major video platform because it operates at a level below the screenshare software entirely. No screenshare tool can capture what it cannot see.

The audio intelligence was the hardest part to get right. The challenge is not just transcribing what was said — it is knowing when to start, when to stop, and how to filter out background noise, your own breathing, and keyboard clicks without cutting off legitimate speech. We spent months tuning this until it felt natural in real interview conditions.

Speed was non-negotiable. We optimised every part of the pipeline so that the first words of an answer appear on your screen as quickly as possible. For most questions, you see results faster than you could type a search query — without any manual steps on your part.
