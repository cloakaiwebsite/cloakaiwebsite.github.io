Frequently Asked Questions

Will the interviewer see CloakAI on their screen when I share mine? +
No. CloakAI is completely invisible to anyone viewing your shared screen. It uses a system-level method built into Windows that prevents the overlay from being captured by any screenshare software. The interviewer on Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet will see absolutely nothing. Only you can see it on your physical monitor.
What if I have to share my "Entire Screen" instead of just a window? +
CloakAI remains invisible even when you share your full desktop. The invisibility works at the screen output level, not the application level. It does not matter whether you share a window, a specific monitor, or your entire screen. The result is the same: no one else can see it.
How does CloakAI hear the interviewer's questions? +
It listens through your microphone or computer audio. By default, it automatically detects when someone is speaking and starts processing. When they stop, it sends the audio for analysis and shows you the answer. You can also switch to manual mode where you hold the Alt key while the question is being asked and release to send it.
What if the question is long or complex? Will the answer still be good? +
Yes. The longer and more complex the question, the more context the AI has to work with, which often improves the answer. The AI streams answers word-by-word so you start seeing the response almost immediately regardless.
What is the context feature and why does it matter? +
Before your interview, you can tell CloakAI things like: "I am interviewing for a senior backend engineer role at a fintech company. The stack is Python, Django, and PostgreSQL. I have 4 years of experience." From that point on, every answer it generates is tailored specifically to your situation.
What happens to my questions and answers after the interview? +
CloakAI automatically stores every question asked and every answer generated during your session. You can download the full session log as a file after the interview. No other interview tool offers this feature.
If I accidentally clear my screen mid-interview, is the answer gone? +
No. CloakAI keeps your last 5 answers in memory throughout the session. Use the navigation arrows in the interface to scroll back through previous answers at any point during the interview.
Can it solve coding challenges shown on a shared screen? +
Yes. Use the screen capture feature. Click the camera icon, and CloakAI takes a screenshot of everything visible on your screen and sends it to the AI for analysis. This works for coding problems, data diagrams, system design prompts, or anything displayed visually.
When do I receive my license key after paying? +
Your license key is generated automatically the moment your payment is confirmed and sent to the email address you provided at checkout. This is fully automated and happens within seconds, at any time of day.
Can I use the same license key on two different computers? +
No. Each license is bound to the first computer it is activated on. If your hardware changes and you genuinely need to reactivate on a new device, contact us at support@cloakai.in.
Does it work on Mac? +
Yes. CloakAI works on Windows and MacOS. We recommend you install the Mac version and test all features yourself.
Is my audio or conversation stored anywhere? +
No. Audio is captured on your device, processed in real time, and immediately discarded after the response is generated. We do not record, store, or have access to any audio or conversation content from your sessions.

CloakAI uses a system-level Windows display method that cannot be captured even by the Windows Snipping Tool or OBS. Parakeet and similar tools often use transparent windows that may still appear as a black or visible rectangle in certain capture scenarios. CloakAI is also significantly cheaper at ₹167/hr vs ₹400+ for competitors. We offer features no competitor currently has: session transcript saving, answer history browse mid-interview, screen capture analysis, and deep context-aware tailoring.

No. The CloakAI overlay cannot be captured by the Windows Snipping Tool, Windows screenshot shortcuts (Win+Shift+S), OBS, or any screen recording software. The exclusion operates below the application layer, at the display driver level.
