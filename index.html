Frequently Asked Questions

Will the interviewer see CloakAI on their screen when I share mine? +
No. CloakAI is completely invisible to anyone viewing your shared screen. It uses a system-level method built into Windows that prevents the overlay from being captured by any screenshare software. The interviewer on Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet will see absolutely nothing. Only you can see it on your physical monitor.
What if I have to share my "Entire Screen" instead of just a window? +
CloakAI remains invisible even when you share your full desktop. The invisibility works at the screen output level, not the application level. It does not matter whether you share a window, a specific monitor, or your entire screen. The result is the same: no one else can see it.
How does CloakAI hear the interviewer's questions? +
It listens through your microphone or computer audio. By default, it automatically detects when someone is speaking and starts processing. When they stop, it sends the audio for analysis and shows you the answer. You can also switch to manual mode where you hold the Alt key while the question is being asked and release to send it. This gives you full control over when it listens.
What if the question is long or complex? Will the answer still be good? +
Yes. The longer and more complex the question, the more context the AI has to work with, which often improves the answer. The response time will naturally be a little longer for complex questions than for short ones, but the AI streams answers word-by-word so you start seeing the response almost immediately regardless.
What is the context feature and why does it matter? +
Before your interview, you can tell CloakAI things like: "I am interviewing for a senior backend engineer role at a fintech company. The stack is Python, Django, and PostgreSQL. I have 4 years of experience." From that point on, every answer it generates is tailored specifically to your situation. Instead of generic responses, you get answers that match the role, the company, and your own experience level. This is one of our most powerful and unique features.
What happens to my questions and answers after the interview? +
CloakAI automatically stores every question asked and every answer generated during your session. You can download the full session log as a file after the interview. This lets you review what came up, how the AI answered, what you can improve, and build a personal reference library over time. No other interview tool offers this feature.
If I accidentally clear my screen mid-interview, is the answer gone? +
No. CloakAI keeps your last 5 answers in memory throughout the session. Use the navigation arrows in the interface to scroll back through previous answers at any point during the interview. This is especially useful when a follow-up question refers to something from earlier in the conversation.
Can it solve coding challenges shown on a shared screen? +
Yes. Use the screen capture feature. Click the camera icon, and CloakAI takes a screenshot of everything visible on your screen and sends it to the AI for analysis. This works for coding problems, data diagrams, system design prompts, or anything displayed visually that you cannot copy as text. You can type a specific question about what's on screen or leave it blank to get a general analysis.

No. Each license is bound to the first computer it is activated on. If you try to use it on a different machine, it will be rejected. This is a security measure to prevent sharing. If your hardware changes and you genuinely need to reactivate on a new device, contact us at support.cloakai@gmail.com.
Does it work on Mac? +
No. CloakAI currently works on Windows 10 and Windows 11 only. The screenshare invisibility relies on Windows-specific system features that do not exist on macOS. A Mac version is something we plan to explore in the future.

No. Audio is captured on your device, processed in real time, and immediately discarded after the response is generated. We do not record, store, or have access to any audio or conversation content from your sessions. The only local file stored is the session transcript on your own computer, which you control.

Can the Snipping Tool or Windows screenshot capture CloakAI's overlay? +
No. This is one of our most important technical properties. The CloakAI overlay cannot be captured by the Windows Snipping Tool, Windows screenshot shortcuts (Win+Shift+S), OBS, or any screen recording software. The exclusion operates below the application layer, at the display driver level. Even if someone were watching your screen sharing and tried to screenshot your shared feed, they would see nothing. The only way to know CloakAI is present is to physically look at your monitor in the room with you. This is fundamentally different from how most competing tools work.
